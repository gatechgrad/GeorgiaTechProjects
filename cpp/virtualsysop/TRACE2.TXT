The Southern Star Bulletin Board System (BBS) was founded in March of 1988.
The system now has 8 lines, runs on 4 CPUs over a LAN and has 25 Gigabytes
of files for download.  All of the dial-in lines use high speed modems,
supporting V.32bis and V.34+.  The rest are Telnet lines.  The software is
eSoft's TBBS.

The BBS was founded to encourage the exchange of technical information and
the distribution of software for IBM-type PCs.  While this is still the main
focus of the BBS, many diverse areas are covered.

The BBS is on the Internet as bbs@sstar.com.  Internet e-mail (mailing lists
and FTP-mail) and newsgroups are available.  Direct Internet access (Telnet,
FTP, Finger, Ping) is also available.  SLIP/PPP feeds are available
separately and are not a part of the BBS's services.  See the Registration
Menu, "Internet SLIP/PPP Service", for information.

The BBS is a member of FidoNet, 1:396/1.  Indeed, it serves as one of the
available along with FidoNet Filebone files.

This is a private BBS.  Feel free to look around, but for full access you
must subscribe.  The main phone number for the BBS is 504-885-5928.
