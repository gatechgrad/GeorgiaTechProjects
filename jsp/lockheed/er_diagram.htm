E-R Diagram 

 

The objective of Lockheeds project is to create a database capable of tracking devices. These devices are capable of reporting their status, tempature, flow rate and so on..
The database should be capable of storing the location of the device. The device supplier and its maintanence records.
The database must be capable of storing future devices and capture its readings which will not be defined until the device is installed.
The database entities are device, system, zone, supplier, maintainer, and measurement type. (measurement type = behavior)
As you can see in the diagram a device can be related to a system by grouping. Devices are logicly grouped into systems. Systems can be related to each other in a hierarcy. With this design a system can be a substem of another system and so on. Another organizational tool lockheed requested was the ability to place devices in a zone. Here you can see a device is related to a zone by the zoning table.
It is also of interest to have the ability to connect a device to another device. In the ER Diagram below you can see that two devices are related by a connection.
And obviously a device is related to a supplier by an installation and a device is related to a maintainer by its maintence records.
The tricky part of the ER Diagram is to allow a device to record its measurements when the measurement types that the device records is unknown until installaion.
We prepose to solve this by relating a device to measurement types with a dynamicdb. The dynamicDB relation stores all the information to properly extract from the runtime created table which will store the devices measurements.
